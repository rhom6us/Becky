# **The Incredible, Unstoppable Zebra of Destiny**

It started, as most great adventures do, with someone hiding under the covers.

His name was Stripes. He was a stuffed zebra of immense ambition and very little common sense. On the morning of The Great Escape, he peeked out from beneath the blankets with the cautious energy of a man who had definitely not thought this through.

*The bedroom was quiet. The boy was asleep. This was his moment.*

Stripes made his move.

---

First stop: the boombox. Stripes had always believed, deep in his polyester soul, that he was meant to be a rock star. He threw himself onto the boombox in what he intended to be a dramatic rock-god pose. A U2 CD was already loaded. Perfect. He spread his little striped arms wide and screamed internally.

No one clapped. The boombox did nothing. This was fine.

---

He found the scissors next. Giant, terrifying scissors sitting on the bed like they owned the place. Stripes seized them immediately. He had no plan for the scissors. He simply felt that a zebra holding scissors commanded *respect*.

He was not wrong.

---

Word spread fast that something was happening in the bedroom. The boy woke up, assessed the situation (his zebra had apparently armed himself), and did the only logical thing: he put Stripes on his head.

The boy stared off into the middle distance with the grave expression of a philosopher considering the nature of existence. Or possibly wondering what was for breakfast. It was hard to tell.

Stripes, perched atop the boy's head like a tiny striped crown, was thrilled.

---

Then came the screaming phase.

Nobody is entirely sure what triggered it. Some historians blame the scissors. Others blame the U2 CD. The boy opened his mouth in a primal howl, the zebra still riding his head like a jockey at the world's most chaotic horse race.

Stripes held on for dear life, which is impressive given that he had no grip strength whatsoever.

---

After the chaos, Stripes needed a moment alone.

He climbed to the top of the bedpost — the highest point in the room — and surveyed his kingdom. The carpet. The laundry basket. The faint orange stain on the wall that nobody ever explained.

*Yes,* he thought. *This is all mine.*

---

Meanwhile, Stripes's roommate — a large fluffy ostrich who went by the name Gerald — had been lying facedown on the carpet since Tuesday. Gerald had no opinions about any of this. Gerald was doing *his best.*

---

The night ended with Stripes draped dramatically across a large white stuffed sheep near the window, staring out into the darkness. The sheep, named Margaret, had not consented to this arrangement but had made peace with it.

"Do you ever wonder," Stripes asked, "if there's more out there for us?"

Margaret said nothing, because she was also a stuffed animal.

Stripes gazed into the void.

The void, wisely, did not gaze back.

**THE END.**
