# **The Incredible, Unstoppable Zebra of Destiny**

It started, as most great adventures do, with someone hiding under the covers.

His name was Stripes. He was a stuffed zebra of immense ambition and very little common sense. On the morning of The Great Escape, he peeked out from beneath the blankets with the cautious energy of a man who had absolutely not thought this through.

> 📸 *[Photo 1 — Stripes peers out from under the white blanket, surveying his kingdom]*

*The bedroom was quiet. The boy was asleep. This was his moment.*

---

First stop: the boombox. Stripes had always believed, deep in his polyester soul, that he was meant to be a rock star. He threw himself onto the boombox in what he intended to be a dramatic rock-god pose. A U2 CD sat right there, already loaded. He spread his little striped arms wide and screamed internally at an imaginary crowd of thousands.

The boombox did nothing. No one clapped.

This was fine.

> 📸 *[Photo 2 — Stripes, legs akimbo, rocking out on the boombox like the legend he believes himself to be]*

---

He found the scissors next. Giant, gleaming, terrifying scissors just sitting on the bed like they owned the place. Stripes seized them immediately. He had no plan for the scissors. He simply felt that a zebra *holding scissors* commanded a certain level of respect.

He posed.

He waited for someone to be impressed.

> 📸 *[Photo 3 — Stripes wielding the scissors with unearned confidence]*

---

Word spread fast that something was happening in the bedroom. The boy woke up, rubbed his eyes, and assessed the situation: his zebra had apparently armed himself and staged some kind of coup.

The boy did the only logical thing. He picked Stripes up and placed him carefully on top of his own head.

Then he sat very still and thought about it.

> 📸 *[Photo 4 — The boy, deeply pensive, with Stripes riding his head like a tiny striped crown]*

---

Nobody is entirely sure what happened next. Some historians blame the scissors. Others blame the U2 CD. Either way, something snapped.

The boy opened his mouth in a full, uninhibited, primal *howl* — the kind of scream that comes not from fear, but from a deep spiritual place where a kid and his zebra have simply transcended the rules of normal behavior entirely.

Stripes held on for dear life.

> 📸 *[Photo 5 — The boy mid-scream, Stripes still aboard, both of them fully committed to the bit]*

---

After the chaos, Stripes needed a moment alone to reflect.

He climbed to the very top of the bedpost — the highest point available to a stuffed animal of his stature — and surveyed everything below him. The carpet. The laundry basket. The faint orange stain on the wall that no one had ever explained.

*Yes,* he thought. *This is all mine.*

> 📸 *[Photo 6 — Stripes, perched atop the bedpost, lord of all he surveys]*

---

Meanwhile, Stripes's roommate Gerald — a large stuffed ostrich — had been lying completely face-down on the carpet since sometime around Tuesday. Gerald had no opinions about any of this. Gerald was simply doing his best.

> 📸 *[Photo 7 — Gerald, facedown, unbothered, deeply committed to the floor]*

---

The night ended with Stripes draped dramatically across Margaret, a large fluffy white stuffed sheep, staring out the dark window at the world beyond the glass.

"Do you ever wonder," Stripes asked softly, "if there's more out there for us?"

Margaret said nothing, because she was also a stuffed animal.

Stripes gazed into the void anyway.

> 📸 *[Photo 8 — Stripes and Margaret, silhouetted against the dark window, contemplating everything]*

---

*The boy slept soundly. The scissors were somewhere on the bed. The boombox never played. Gerald remained on the floor.*

*It had been, by any measure, a perfect day.*

**THE END.**
