## New Findings

### 1. The "quiet, patient" echo may be approaching saturation *(deliberate-risk note, not a flag)*

**Location:** Lines 74, 92, 120, 196.

**Observation:** The phrase "quiet, patient" (and variants: "quietly, patiently") appears four times. Each describes the corruption's character. The corridor instance (196) is the one that carries the most weight — it is the moment where the phrase turns inward, from something that happens to other people to something that is happening to Tom. Four instances across a short story is a high density. By the time the reader reaches the corridor, the phrase may scan as established diction rather than landing as a new and unsettling self-application.

**Note:** The current density is defensible. The question is whether reducing from four to three would free the corridor version to register as a sharper turn. Author's call.

### 2. "Cold water" simile recurs in close proximity *(line-editor referral)*

**Location:** Line 120: *"He'd felt the corruption move through him like cold water."* Line 130: *"forty seconds of music moving through him like cold water."*

**Observation:** The identical simile appears ten lines apart. The structural logic of both instances is sound — sensation versus analysis. The issue is limited to phrasing proximity. **Refer to line editor.**

### 3. Avoidance backstory stated twice in near-identical phrasing *(line-editor referral)*

**Location:** Line 40: *"He'd steered clear of anything that smelled like it for months."* Line 80: *"He'd been avoiding anything that smelled like this for months."*

**Observation:** Unlike the story's deliberate echoes (which modulate between instances), this repetition does not change in form or meaning. The structural function is clear — line 40 establishes the avoidance, line 80 reactivates it at the moment of decision. But the phrasing does not differentiate the two moments. **Refer to line editor** to consider whether line 80 can lean on the reader's memory of line 40 rather than restating in full.

### 4. Structural strengths documented for the record

**The "filed it" progression (lines 50, 118):** "Tom filed it away — the thing he couldn't name" becomes "He filed it next to the other things he couldn't name." The single unnamed thing becomes a collection. The deduction scene is where that collection assembles into meaning. Quiet and effective.

**The deduction's transparency to attentive readers (lines 128–134):** Every piece of evidence Tom assembles has been shown to the reader at the moment it occurred. An attentive first-time reader can construct the deduction before or alongside Tom. The story works for both the reader who sees it coming and the one who doesn't. Core strength; do not alter.

**The "Tom looked at him" progression (lines 118, 166, 190):** "For a moment" → "for a long time" → "until he was done looking." The progression compresses as Tom's capacity for processing diminishes.
